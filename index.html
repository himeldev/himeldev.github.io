Bio

I am a 4th year PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign (UIUC). My research interests lie in the area of Data Science and its intersection with Economics. I work with Prof. Hari Sundaram on mining and modeling social media. We study the successes and failures of these platforms through the lens of Economics. I have also worked with Prof. Aditya Parameswaran on visual analytics—developing an interactive visual summarization system. I have worked as a research intern at Visa Research (Summer 2018), and Adobe Research (Summer 2016).

In my previous life, I was a lecturer in Computer Science and Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), having completed my B.Sc. from the same department. I received several honors for my undergraduate research including The Undergraduate Awards [world's largest academic awards program for undergraduates], invited young researcher at Heidelberg Laureate Forum [prestigious meeting with the Abel, Fields, and Turing Laureates], and National ICT Achievement Award [prestigious recognition in ICT by the Government of the People's Republic of Bangladesh].

Research


Internship Projects
In this project, I designed a novel outlier-aware frequent pattern mining method that extracts user tasks from application logs. The method achieved 1.4X improvement over baseline in identifying user tasks from Photoshop logs. The outcome of this project includes two publications (ACM IUI 2017, IEEE VIS Companion 2016).


In this project, I developed an end-to-end framework for inferring the cuisine types of restaurants from debit/credit card transactions. The framework achieved 1.5X improvement over baseline in identifying the cuisine types of US restaurants from Visa card transactions. The expected outcome of this project includes a publication (currently under review).
